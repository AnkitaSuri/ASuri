ABOUT ME

Hi, I’m Ankita! I work in non-profit program management, run a book club that’s been meeting in Thai restaurants since 2011, and was the winner of the Glen Oaks Elementary School “Where In The World is Carmen Sandiego?” competition in 1999.

As a child, my family was certain I’d be a lawyer; I’d spend holidays debating animal welfare and human rights with anyone who’d listen. Like many other children of immigrants, it took me a quite a while to realize that I could choose my own career. Peculiarly, an episode of Anthony Bourdain’s TV show (No Reservations) helped turned my path. I was fascinated when he visited Beirut during the Lebanon July War in 2006, and it struck my personal interest in international politics and the Middle East. Knowing I had a passion for advocacy, I studied Political Science, Sociology, and International Relations at CUNY Baruch College, graduating in 2011. As an adult, I found my calling: philanthropy and enriching civil society. Whether working on a mobile bus classroom in New Delhi or coordinating an international scholarship program in New York, I know I am following my passion and helping others.

Currently, I work at All Star Code as a Program Manager where I manage a Summer Intensive program for young men of color to learn computer science and entrepreneurship skills. In my role, I focus on program design, expansion and monitoring and evaluation.

In my free time, I've participated in opportunities to continue learning through volunteering, advisory groups and consulting. Reach out to me if you have any career or consulting opportunities, or recommendations for Thai restaurants!
